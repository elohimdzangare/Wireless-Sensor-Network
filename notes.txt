
**ESP32 LED Debugging Session — Summary**

**Setup:**
- ESP32 (regular, non-NodeMCU) connected via USB to laptop
- LED + resistor wired to GPIO27, grounded to breadboard common ground rail

---

**Issues Encountered & Ruled Out**

| Suspect | Outcome |
|---------|---------|
| Wrong GPIO pin (D14 vs GPIO14) | Ruled out — board uses GPIO numbers directly |
| LED polarity reversed | Briefly confused by negative multimeter reading, confirmed correct orientation eventually |
| Resistor value too high | Ruled out — 470Ω and 1kΩ both tried |
| Dead LED | Ruled out — lit up fine on 6V external supply |
| ESP32 not outputting voltage | Ruled out — GPIO27 measured at 3.26V directly on pin |
| Faulty jumper wire | Ruled out |
| ESP32 underpowered via USB | Ruled out |
| Resistor not making breadboard contact | Suspected but turned out to be a red herring |

---

**Root Cause**

The breadboard ground rail has a **physical gap splitting it into two electrically independent halves.** The ESP32 GND pin was connected to one half, and the resistor's ground leg was in the other half — meaning they were never actually connected despite appearing to be on the same continuous rail.

---

**Fix**

Bridged the two halves of the ground rail with a short jumper wire. LED lit up immediately.

---

**Key Lessons**
- Always bridge both halves of breadboard power rails (GND and VCC) with a jumper wire at the start of any new build
- When an LED won't light, probe each node in the chain methodically with a multimeter rather than swapping components
- No voltage drop across a resistor = no current flowing = broken connection somewhere in the circuit
- GPIO27 was briefly shorted (44mA measured) — ESP32 GPIOs are rated for 12mA max, avoid leaving LEDs connected without a current limiting resistor
